Tax Saving Options

1. Tax-Saving Instruments under Section 80C

Section 80C allows individuals to claim deductions of up to ₹1.5 lakh on the total income. Popular options include:

  • Equity-Linked Savings Scheme (ELSS): ELSS funds offer deductions up to ₹1.5 lakh with a 3-year lock-in.
  • Public Provident Fund (PPF): Government-backed with tax-free interest and eligible for deductions.
  • National Pension System (NPS): Provides deductions up to ₹1.5 lakh, plus an extra ₹50,000 under Section 80CCD(1B).
  • Life Insurance Premiums: Premiums for life insurance, including ULIPs, qualify for tax deductions.
  • Employee Provident Fund (EPF): EPF contributions qualify for tax deductions under Section 80C.
  • 5-Year Bank Fixed Deposit: Deductions are allowed on 5-year deposits, though interest earned is taxable.

2. Section 80D: Health Insurance Premiums

Tax deductions on health insurance premiums include:

  • ₹25,000 for individuals under 60 years of age.
  • ₹50,000 for senior citizens aged 60 years or above.
  • Additional deductions apply for parents' health insurance premiums.

3. Section 80E: Education Loan Interest

Deductions are allowed for interest on loans for higher education with no cap on the interest amount and up to 8 years or until repayment.

4. Section 24(b): Home Loan Interest

An exemption of up to ₹2 lakh on home loan interest for self-occupied properties. Interest on rented property loans is fully deductible.

5. Section 10(10D): Life Insurance Maturity

Maturity proceeds are tax-free under certain conditions related to the premium amount relative to the sum assured.

6. Section 80G: Charitable Donations

Donations to registered charitable institutions qualify for deductions of 50% or 100%, depending on the organization.

7. Section 54: Property Sale Capital Gains

Exemption is available on capital gains if reinvested in another residential property.

8. Section 54EC: Investment in Bonds

Exemption on capital gains if invested in specific bonds within six months of sale, with a limit of ₹50 lakh.

9. Tax-Free Investments

Some tax-free investment options include:

  • Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGBs): Capital gains are exempt upon redemption.
  • Tax-Free Bonds: Bonds issued by government entities offer tax-free interest income.

10. Tax-Free Dividend Income

Dividends from Indian companies are exempt under Section 10(34), though dividends exceeding ₹5,000 are taxable.

11. Section 80U: Deductions for Disabilities

Deductions of ₹75,000 for normal disability and ₹1,25,000 for severe disability (80% or more).

12. Additional Tax Saving Measures

Other tax-saving options include:

  • House Rent Allowance (HRA): Exemptions based on rent paid and salary.
  • Standard Deduction: ₹50,000 for salaried and pensioned individuals.

Conclusion

Tax-saving goes beyond reducing tax bills—it's about securing your financial future through informed planning.
